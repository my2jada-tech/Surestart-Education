üî§ Section 3: Understanding Suffixes

What is a Suffix?

A suffix is a word part added to the END of a word to change its meaning or function.

Suffix Meaning Examples
-ible/-able able to, can be visible (can be seen), comfortable (can be comfort)
-ance/-ence state or quality of importance, confidence, appearance
-ant/-ent person who, or showing servant (person who serves), different (showing difference)
-ous full of dangerous (full of danger), famous (full of fame)
-less without careless (without care), helpless (without help)
-ful full of careful (full of care), helpful (full of help)
-ment act or process enjoyment (act of enjoying), government
-ness state of being kindness (state of being kind), happiness
Practice Examples:
comfort + able = comfortable
care + less = careless
danger + ous = dangerous
enjoy + ment = enjoyment
help + ful = helpful
govern + ment = government

‚ö†Ô∏è Section 5: Spelling Changes with Suffixes

Important Spelling Rules

Rule 1: Change Y to I
When a word ends in consonant + y, change the y to i before adding a suffix
Examples: happy + ness = happiness, busy + ness = business
Rule 2: Double the Final Consonant
When a one-syllable word ends in consonant-vowel-consonant, double the last letter
Examples: swim + ing = swimming, run + ing = running
Rule 3: Drop the Silent E
When adding a suffix that starts with a vowel, drop the silent e
Examples: create + ion = creation, argue + ment = argument
Base Word Suffix Correct Spelling Rule Applied

swim + ing swimming double the m
create + ion creation drop the e
busy + ness business y changes to i
argue + ment argument drop the e
üí≠ Section 6: Abstract Nouns

What are Abstract Nouns?

Abstract nouns name things you CANNOT touch or see with your eyes. They are ideas, feelings, or qualities.

Concrete Nouns (you CAN touch): book, car, dog, tree
Abstract Nouns (you CANNOT touch): love, happiness, courage, freedom

Common Abstract Nouns to Know:

  • Feelings: love, anger, joy, sadness, fear, happiness
  • Qualities: kindness, honesty, bravery, beauty, generosity, wisdom
  • Ideas: freedom, justice, peace, success, knowledge, truth
  • States: childhood, friendship, courage, patience, dedication
Practice Sentences:
1. Her kindness and generosity touched everyone's hearts.
2. The children showed great courage during the storm.
3. Success requires patience, dedication, and wisdom.
4. Their friendship has lasted through many difficulties.
5. The beauty of nature fills us with joy and peace.
üìù Section 7: Creating Noun Phrases

What is a Noun Phrase?

A noun phrase is a group of words that includes a noun and words that describe it (adjectives, articles, etc.)

Simple Noun: car
Noun Phrase: the shiny red sports car

Simple Noun: dog
Noun Phrase: the friendly golden retriever with a wagging tail

Simple Noun: house
Noun Phrase: the old Victorian house on the hill

How to Build Great Noun Phrases:

  1. Start with a noun
  2. Add adjectives (describing words)
  3. Add articles (a, an, the)
  4. Add prepositional phrases (on the hill, in the sky)
Practice: Take boring sentences and make them interesting!
BORING: The teacher helped us.
BETTER: The patient, experienced teacher with the kind smile helped us.
üî† Section 8: Understanding Prefixes

What is a Prefix?

A prefix is a word part added to the BEGINNING of a word to change its meaning.

Prefix Meaning Examples
un- not, opposite of unhappy, unclear, unethical
in- not, without incomplete, infrequent, intolerant
im- not (before m, p, b) impatient, impossible, immobile
il- not (before l) illegal, illegible, illogical
ir- not (before r) irresponsible, irregular, irrelevant
dis- not, opposite of dishonest, disagree, disappear

Practice with Prefixes

Base Word Add Prefix New Word Meaning
patient im- impatient not patient
responsible ir- irresponsible not responsible
legal il- illegal not legal
honest dis- dishonest not honest
frequent in- infrequent not frequent
legible il- illegible not legible (can't be read)
tolerant in- intolerant not tolerant
ethical un- unethical not ethical
More Examples:
inefficient = not efficient, wasteful
unconscious = not conscious, not aware
irregular = not regular, uneven
immobile = not mobile, unable to move
üå≥ Section 9: Root Words and Word Families

What is a Root Word?

A root word is the basic word without any prefixes or suffixes. All related words come from the same root.

Given Word Root Word Related Words
dishonest honest honesty, honestly, dishonesty
musician music musical, musically
examination examine exam, examiner, examining
explorer explore exploration, exploring, exploratory
producer produce production, product, productive
Word Family Example - HAPPY:
Root: happy
Family: unhappy, happiness, happily, happier, happiest
